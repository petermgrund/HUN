## Electoral system

The City Council consists of 31 elected members who serve four-year terms. Members are elected to a four-year term in parallel voting, with 22 members elected in single-seat constituencies and 9 members elected by proportional representation. This means that voters cast two votes: one for a candidate in their single-seat constituency and one for a party list in the proportional representation part of the election.


The voting in the elections is open to all citizens of Tyrvhuln who are at least 18 years old. 

Because only one member is elected in each single-seat constituency, not all parties run candidates in all constituencies. The proportional representation seats are allocated based on the total number of votes each party receives in the proportional representation part of the election.

The right-of-centre parties ([Initsikat](/HUN/about/party/initsikat)) contest seats under the name Tyrvhuln Coalition, which is an alliance of Natural Balance, A Nation to Preserve, and Farmers and Fishers.

### Heimili plass C

Heimili plass C covers the eastern third of the Heimili plass neighborhood. It is the most racially diverse district in Tyrvhuln. The district also had the largest number of candidates, with 6 parties and 2 unaffiliated candidates running.

Incumbent Jorundr Eldfinnrsundr of the Tyrvhuln Coalition (Natural Balance) narrowly lost to Jakub Kowalski of the Liberals. There was significant vote splitting among the parties compared to the previous election, where Natural Balance won with 45% of the vote. Jakub Kowalski became one of the first two Polish-born candidates (along with Mateusz Wójcik) to win a seat on the council.

### Ny kross B

Ny kross B covers the eastern half of Ny kross. It includes the University of Hverland campus and the surrounding student housing. The district has been represented by the Greens and Futurists party since 2004. Kjora Snygghildrdotra won the election with 27.61% of the vote, replacing the retiring Kjeld Ragnarssundr, also of the Greens and Futurists. This was the lowest vote share for the Greens and Futurists in Ny kross B since 2004, and also the lowest vote share for any winning candidate this election. Yannis Xenides, a Greek-Hverlandic student, received 23.49% of the vote. He was the first Communist Left candidate to run in a Tyrvhuln municipal election. His campaign focused on student issues and improved wages for teaching assistants. The Spirit of Innovation party candidate, Ordmar Gunnarsundr, was the victim of a physical assault by an unknown assailant during the campaign.
